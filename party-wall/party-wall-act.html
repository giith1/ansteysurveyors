Key Components of a Party Wall Award:

  • Scope of Work: A detailed outline of the proposed construction activities, such as excavation, underpinning, or modifications to the party wall.
  • Timeline and Access: The projected schedule for the work, including any specific conditions or restrictions related to accessing the adjoining property.
  • Protective Measures: Safety precautions to protect the neighboring property, such as shoring, monitoring systems, or other preventive measures.
  • Dispute Resolution: Defined procedures for handling disputes, including the option to appoint a third surveyor if disagreements occur.
  • Compensation: Guidelines for compensating the adjoining owner for any damage resulting from the construction work.

What Happens After an Award is Served?

Once an Award is served, the following steps must be taken:

The works must adhere to the agreed terms outlined in the Award.
Post-work assessments may be carried out to verify compliance and document any changes to neighboring properties.
While not legally required under the Party Wall etc. Act 1996, a schedule of condition is strongly recommended for anyone undertaking construction near shared boundaries.

This document provides a detailed record of the adjoining property's condition before work begins. Its primary purpose is to serve as evidence in case of any damage during construction. By documenting the original state of the property, it enables both parties to fairly assess and address any potential issues.

Although optional, a schedule of condition benefits both the building owner and the adjoining owner. It helps prevent disputes by offering clear, impartial documentation and facilitates efficient resolution of any concerns.

For these reasons, arranging a schedule of condition before starting construction—even when a party wall notice has been agreed upon—is a prudent step. Party wall surveyors can assist in preparing this document, ensuring transparency and fostering smoother communication between all involved parties.
